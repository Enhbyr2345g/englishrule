Noun

A noun is a word used to refer diveople, animal, object, substances, states, ideas, and feelings. A noun functions as a subject or object of a verb and can be modified by an adjective.

Example: John, lion, table, freedom, love
  • I love in United States America.
  • Emma is my sister.
  • I love to play with my cat.

Pronoun

A pronoun is used in the in the place of a noun or phrase. Or Pronouns are words that substitute a noun or another pronoun.

Example: I, you, he, she, it, who, themselves
  • This house belongs to us.
  • I won the race.
  • The matter is between Olivia and me.

Verbs

A verb in syntax is a part of speech which conveys action (bring, read, walk, run, learn) or state of being (exist, stand) or Verb have traditionally been defined as words that show action or state of being.

Sentences in English have a main verb which is stated in tense (Simple present, simple past, simple future...).

Example:
  • William writes short stories at home.
  • Emma is buying a newspaper today.
  • Student went to the playing ground often.

Adjective

An adjective is a word which modifies a noun or a pronoun. Adjectives are describing words.Large, grey, and friendlly are all example of adjectives.

Example:
  • They live in big, beautiful.
  • This shop is much nicer.
  • The sky was multicolored.

Adverbs

An adverb is a word that modifiesverbs, adjectives and other Adverbs. "Modifies" means "tells more about". An adverb tell more about how the verb is being done. Many adverbs end in "-ly".

Example:
  • Emma writes quickly and well.
  • The midwives waited patiently through a long labor.
  • The brother were badly injured in the fight.

Prepositions

A preposition is usually a shoort word placed before a substantive (or pronoun) abd indicating the relation of that substantive to other parts of the sentences (mostly verbs).

Example: in, of, at, by, for, with, under, above, into, onto, upon, about, behind, beside, before, after, towards, inside, outside, below, around, etc.
  • She sat on chair.
  • There is some fruits in the fridge.
  • He was hinding under the roof

Conjunctions

The conjunction is the part of speech used as a "joiner" for words, phrases, or clauses in a particular sentenses

Example: For, and, nor, but, or, yet, so (Fanboys)
  • Pizza and burgers are my favorite snacks.
  • Emma did not like bananas but she loved apples.
  • You may bring a main dish or a side dish to the party.

Interjection

in grammer, an interjection is part of speech or (a laxical categoty) whitch is used to show a short sudden expression of emotion. Interjections are exclamations like Oh!, Um or Ah! They have no real grammatical value but we them quite often, usualy more in speaking than in writing. An interjection is a sjort word or phrase that shows emotion. It often stands alone, unconnected to a sentence.

Example:
  • Oops! I did it again!
  • Ooh, what a lovely dress!
  • Alas, it was not to be.
  • You two ate dating? Awesome!
  • Bingo! Right on target!
  • Oh wow, that is so cool!

Determiners

Determiners are words that introduce nouns and specify their meaning in terms of quantity, proximity, definiteness, or possession.

Example: A, an, the, this, these, those, some, many etc.
  • My house is being renovated.
  • This movie is interesting.
  • I bought some apples and mangoes.
